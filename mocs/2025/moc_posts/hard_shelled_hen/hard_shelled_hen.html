Hard-Shelled Hen

While in Denmark, I picked up one of the best elements ever produced by Lego: a plush chicken head. Of course, this element doesn't have any connections, but when has that ever been a problem? With this still being a Rogue Olympics build, the 101 part limit still applies. Therefore, with the chicken head as the main inspiration piece, I decided to go big and utilize larger elements this week.

Working at a larger scale with a part limit posed an interesting challenge. While these parts cover a lot of space, most of the part allocation ended up going into the internals to hold up these large pieces. The shell - an upscaled hard hat - was connected by creating a Technic structure to fit around the circumference of the piece. I ended up going through a few iterations to optimize and reduce the amount of parts in the frame.

The final count was 96 pieces. It is a little unfinished on the camera unfriendly side in order to stay under the part limit (and I didn't have enough parts to finish the last few legs). I've been enjoying this sort of "small" build every week, so I guess we'll see what next week offers!
